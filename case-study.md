 Case Study: ATM Card Fraud (Phishing Attack)
 Scenario:

A college student received an SMS claiming to be from his bank. The message said his ATM card would be blocked if he didn’t update his details immediately. The link in the message looked genuine.

He clicked the link and entered his ATM card number, PIN, and OTP.

 What Happened:

Within a few minutes, money was deducted from his bank account without his permission. The student realized he had been a victim of a phishing scam.

 Cybercrime Type:
Phishing
Online Financial Fraud
 Mistakes Made:
Trusted an unknown message
Clicked on a suspicious link
Shared sensitive information (PIN & OTP)
 Prevention Tips:
Never share OTP or PIN with anyone
Avoid clicking unknown or suspicious links
Always check official bank websites/apps
Enable SMS/email alerts for transactions
Report immediately to bank and cybercrime portal
Conclusion:

This case shows how lack of awareness can lead to financial loss. Being careful and informed is the best defense against cybercrime.
